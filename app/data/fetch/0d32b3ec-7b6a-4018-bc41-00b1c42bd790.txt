<p><b>Marek Papszun</b> (born 8 August 1974) is a <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Poland">Polish</a> professional <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Association_football">football</a> <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Manager_(association_football)">manager</a>, who is the current manager of <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Ekstraklasa">Ekstraklasa</a> club <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Raków_Częstochowa">Raków Częstochowa</a>.</p><h2 id="Career">Career</h2><p>In 2010, Papszun was appointed manager of Polish sixth division side <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/KS_Łomianki">KS Łomianki</a>, helping them achieve promotion to the Polish fifth division.[<i>citation needed</i>] In 2011, he was appointed manager of <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Legionovia_Legionowo">Legionovia Legionowo</a> in the Polish <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/III_liga">fourth division</a>, helping them achieve promotion to the Polish <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/II_liga">third division</a>.[<i>citation needed</i>] In 2016, he was appointed manager of Polish third division club <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Raków_Częstochowa">Raków Częstochowa</a>, helping them achieve promotion to the Polish <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Ekstraklasa">Ekstraklasa</a> within 3 seasons and receiving interest from <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Legia_Warsaw">Legia</a>, the most successful club in Poland.</p><p><a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Raków_Częstochowa">Raków Częstochowa</a> led by Marek Papszun won its first major trophy on 2 May 2021, defeating <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Arka_Gdynia">Arka Gdynia</a> in the final of the <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/2020–21_Polish_Cup">Polish Cup</a> by a score of 2–1. The win also secured them a place in the newly formed <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/UEFA_Europa_Conference_League">UEFA Europa Conference League</a> for the <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/2021–22_UEFA_Europa_Conference_League">2021–22 season</a>, their maiden appearance in <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/UEFA_competitions">European football</a>. In the same season, Papszun's Raków finished in second place in the <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/2020–21_Ekstraklasa">Ekstraklasa</a> standings, club's highest ever league position. After the season, he was announced the <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Ekstraklasa">Ekstraklasa</a> Coach of the Year.</p><h2 id="Honours">Honours</h2><h3 id="Manager">Manager</h3><p><b>Raków Częstochowa</b></p><ul><li><a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/I_liga">I liga</a>: <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/2018–19_I_liga">2018–19</a></li><li><a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/II_liga">II liga</a>: <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/2016–17_II_liga">2016–17</a></li><li><a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Polish_Cup">Polish Cup</a>: <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/2020–21_Polish_Cup">2020–21</a></li><li><a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Polish_SuperCup">Polish SuperCup</a>: <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/2021_Polish_SuperCup">2021</a></li></ul><p><b>Individual</b></p><ul><li><a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Piłka_nożna_magazine_plebiscite">Polish Coach of the Year</a>: 2020</li><li><a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Ekstraklasa">Ekstraklasa</a> Coach of the Year: <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/2020–21_Ekstraklasa">2020–21</a></li></ul>
