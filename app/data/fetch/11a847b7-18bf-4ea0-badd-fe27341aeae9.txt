<p class="mw-empty-elt"></p><p><b>Vancouver station</b> is an <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Amtrak">Amtrak</a> intercity rail station in <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Vancouver,_Washington">Vancouver, Washington</a>, <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/United_States">United States</a>. The station is served by four daily round trips of the <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Amtrak_Cascades"><i>Cascades</i></a>, the daily <i><a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Coast_Starlight">Coast Starlight</a></i>, and the daily Portland section of the <i><a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Empire_Builder">Empire Builder</a></i>.</p><p>Vancouver station is located in a <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Wye_(railroad)">wye</a> between the <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Fallbridge_Subdivision">Fallbridge Subdivision</a> and <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Seattle_Subdivision">Seattle Subdivision</a>, just north <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Burlington_Northern_Railroad_Bridge_9.6">Burlington Northern Railroad Bridge 9.6</a> over the Columbia River. A side platform and narrow island platform on the west side of the station building serve the <i>Cascades</i> and <i>Coast Starlight</i> on the Seattle Subdivision, while a side platform to the east serves the <i>Empire Builder</i> on the Fallbridge Subdivision.</p><p>The station was constructed in 1907–08 by the <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Northern_Pacific_Railroad">Northern Pacific Railroad</a> to serve trains using the new Columbia River bridge. Service was transferred to Amtrak in 1971. A partial restoration of the station building took place in 1988, followed by a full renovation in 2008–09.</p>
