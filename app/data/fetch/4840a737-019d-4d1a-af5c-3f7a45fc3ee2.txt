<p><b>Frasin</b> (<a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/German_language">German</a>: <i>Frassin</i>) is a town in <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Suceava_County">Suceava County</a>, mountainous north-eastern Romania. It is situated in the historical region of <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Bukovina">Bukovina</a>. Frasin is the thirteenth-largest urban settlement in the county, with a population of 5,702 according to the 2011 census. It was declared a town in 2004, along with seven other localities in Suceava County. The town administers the former village of Bucșoaia (which became a neighborhood in 2004), Doroteia and Plutonița (with the status of associated villages).</p><h2 id="Administration_and_local_politics">Administration and local politics</h2><h3 id="Town_council">Town council</h3><p>The town's current local council has the following political composition, according to the results of the <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/2020_Romanian_local_elections">2020 Romanian local elections</a>:</p><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2><p>Frasin is surrounded by the <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Ridges_of_Bukovina">Bukovina Ridges</a> of the Obcinele Mari, on the banks of <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Moldova_River">Moldova River</a>, between <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Câmpulung_Moldovenesc">Câmpulung Moldovenesc</a> and <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Gura_Humorului">Gura Humorului</a>, on <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/European_route_E58">European route E58</a>. The town of <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Gura_Humorului">Gura Humorului</a> is only 7 km away. Frasin is connected to the Romanian national railway system and has a <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Railway_station">railway station</a> on the <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Suceava">Suceava</a>–<a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Vatra_Dornei">Vatra Dornei</a> railway.</p><h2 id="History">History</h2><p>In 1785, Frasin was known as a hamlet. One big step towards good development for Frasin took place in 1816 when a potassium factory was built. Frasin was declared a town in 1850.</p><p>Until 1918, Frasin was part of the <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Austrian_monarchy">Austrian monarchy</a> (the province of Bukovina remained an Austrian crown land (<a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/German_language">German</a>: <i>Kronland</i>) after the <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Compromise_of_1867">compromise of 1867</a>), in the <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Câmpulung_Moldovenesc">Câmpulung</a> district, one of the 9 <i>Bezirkshauptmannschaften</i> of the province.</p><h2 id="Tourism">Tourism</h2><p>Frasin is located in the historical region of Bukovina where visitors can admire a whole number of <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Painted_churches_of_northern_Moldavia">painted medieval churches</a> with <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/World_Heritage_Sites">World Heritage status</a>. One of the most popular is <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Voroneț_Monastery">Voroneț Monastery</a>, built in 1488. Another asset of the area are the <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Nature_reserve">nature reserves</a> like the <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Old-growth_forest">primeval forest</a> of <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Stulpicani">Slătioara</a> ('Codrul secular Slătioara') and the forests of the <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Giumalău_Mountains">Giumalău Mountains</a>.</p><h2 id="Natives">Natives</h2><ul><li><a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Radu_Mareș">Radu Mareș</a></li></ul>
