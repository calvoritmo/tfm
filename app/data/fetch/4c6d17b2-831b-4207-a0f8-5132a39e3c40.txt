Its horns are each divided near their base into two prongs of roughly equal length.</p><p>The genus survived until about 12,000 years ago, and was present when <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Paleo-Indians">Paleo-Indians</a> reached North America.</p><p>One of the co-discoverers of <i>S. onusrosagris</i> was <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Quentin_Roosevelt_II">Quentin Roosevelt II</a>, grandson of <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Theodore_Roosevelt">Theodore Roosevelt</a>; he was 14 at the time of the discovery.</p>
