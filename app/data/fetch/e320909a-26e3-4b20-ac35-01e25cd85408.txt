<p><b>George H. Shanley</b> was an architect of <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Great_Falls,_Montana">Great Falls, Montana</a>.</p><p>According to a 1920 <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Bismarck,_North_Dakota">Bismarck, North Dakota</a> newspaper article, Shanley was a specialist in designing newspaper publishing plants.  He designed works from 'education, commercial, residential, and hotel buildings to concrete arch bridges.'  He worked alone and with partners.</p><p>A number of his works are listed on the U.S. <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/National_Register_of_Historic_Places">National Register of Historic Places</a>.</p><p>Works include (with attribution):</p><ul><li><a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Bismarck_Tribune_Building">Bismarck Tribune Building</a>, 22 N. 4th St. <a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Bismarck,_ND">Bismarck, ND</a> (Shanley, George H.), NRHP-listed</li><li><a href="http://dbpedia.org/resource/Graves_Hotel">Graves Hotel</a>, (1908-1909), 106 S.
